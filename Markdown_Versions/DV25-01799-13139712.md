Opposition To Motion To Strike
COMES NOW, Plaintiff, DONOVAN CLARK, in Proper Person, and respectfully submits this
Opposition to the Defendant’s Motion to Strick [sic], filed on December 26, 2025. This Opposition
is based on the following Memorandum of Points and Authorities and the pleadings and papers
on file herein.
MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES
I. INTRODUCTION On December 26, 2025, the Defendant filed a document titled "Motion to
Strick" (Transaction #11423416). The Motion requests that the Court strike "improper, duplicative,
and irrelevant filings" but fails to identify a single specific document by Title, Date, or Transaction
Number. Furthermore, the Motion contains a sworn statement that is demonstrably false
regarding the existence of the minor child. The Motion is procedurally defective and must be
denied.
II. ARGUMENT
A. The Motion is Void for Vagueness and Violates Due Process. The Defendant requests the
Court to strike "filings" and "documents" generally (See Motion, Sec. 2). She fails to cite a single
specific Transaction Number or Date of Filing. Nevada law requires that a party be given
"reasonable advance notice of an issue to be raised and an opportunity to respond
1

---


(Anastassatos v. Anastassatos, 112 Nev. 317). By failing to specify which documents she seeks
to strike, the Defendant makes it impossible for the Plaintiff to defend the validity of his evidence.
The Plaintiff cannot respond to a claim that has not been defined. Furthermore, the Court cannot
grant the relief requested because the Clerk of the Court requires specific instructions to seal or
strike documents. A blanket request to "strike filings" places an improper burden on the Court to
review the entire docket and guess the Defendant’s intent.
B. The Motion Contains Material Falsehoods Under Penalty of Perjury. The Defendant
signed her Motion under penalty of perjury. On Page 1, Section 1, she explicitly marked the box
stating:
"There are no minor children involved in this matter." [Transaction #11423416, Page 1]
Immediately below this check-box, she listed the minor child, "Alexis Clark, 11." This
contradiction is fatal to the credibility of her filing. This Court has jurisdiction over the custody of
the minor child precisely because this is a domestic relations matter involving children. If the
Defendant cannot accurately attest to the existence of the minor child in a sworn court filing, her
assertions regarding the "irrelevance" of the Plaintiff’s evidence cannot be trusted.
C. The Evidence of Bigamy is Material to Annulment. The Plaintiff initiated this action for
Annulment based on the allegation that the marriage is Void Ab Initio due to Bigamy (NRS
125.290). The Plaintiff has filed public records, including a Notice of Booking (Transaction
#11390164, filed Dec 07, 2025), documenting the Defendant’s arrest for Bigamy. The Defendant
attempts to label these filings as "harassment" or "irrelevant." In an Annulment proceeding,
evidence that the Defendant has been charged with Bigamy is the central material fact of the
case. It is not harassment to provide the Court with evidence of the specific fraud alleged in the
Complaint.
III. CONCLUSION The Defendant’s Motion is vague, fails to identify specific targets for striking,
and contains perjured statements regarding the minor child. WHEREFORE, Plaintiff requests
that the Defendant’s Motion to Strike be DENIED.
